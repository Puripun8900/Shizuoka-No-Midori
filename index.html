Obuchi Tea Fields - Where Mount Fuji Meets Premium Tea

The Most Photographed Tea Fields in Japan

Obuchi Sasaba in Fuji City is the most popular scenic spot where you can see both Mount Fuji and tea fields simultaneously. This iconic location attracts photographers from around the world who come to capture the perfect shot of tea gardens with Mount Fuji in the background.

What makes Obuchi special is the unobstructed view - no power lines or buildings interfere with the pristine landscape. The best viewing season is from late April to early October, when the tea fields are at their most vibrant green.

Our tea comes from these very fields that have inspired countless artists and photographers.

Honyama Premium Matcha - Spring Winter Tea

First Flush Excellence - Ichiban-cha

Our premium matcha comes from the first harvest of spring (April-May), known as "Ichiban-cha" or "Spring Winter Tea." During winter, tea plants store nutrients, and when combined with Mount Fuji's mineral-rich snowmelt water, they produce leaves with exceptional aroma, taste, and concentrated tea essence.

Each leaf is hand-picked by skilled artisans who select only uniform-sized young shoots from tea plants in Honyama's high-altitude gardens. This traditional method, passed down through generations, ensures the highest quality matcha powder.

Experience the concentrated essence of Shizuoka's sacred tea mountains in every cup.

Traditional Tea Picking Heritage

Preserving Ancient Traditions

Our tea pickers wear traditional Japanese clothing and use time-honored techniques passed down through generations. This careful hand-picking method ensures only the finest young leaves are selected for our premium teas.

The traditional approach not only maintains quality but also preserves the cultural heritage of Shizuoka tea farming. Each picker is trained to identify the perfect leaves that will create the most flavorful and aromatic tea.

Every cup tells the story of centuries-old Japanese tea craftsmanship.

About Shizuoka Tea

The Heart of Japanese Tea Culture

Shizuoka Prefecture is the heart of Japanese tea culture, producing over 40% of Japan's tea. Located at the foot of Mount Fuji, our region's unique climate, with its mild temperatures, adequate rainfall, and mountainous terrain, creates the perfect conditions for growing exceptional tea.

Honyama: Our Sacred Tea Mountains

The Honyama region, nestled high in Shizuoka's mountains, is where we source our finest matcha. This sacred area, shrouded in morning mist and blessed by Mount Fuji's presence, has been cultivating premium tea for over 800 years. The high altitude and pristine environment produce matcha of unparalleled quality and depth.

Our Heritage

We work directly with local tea farmers who have been perfecting their craft for generations. Many of our partner farms in Honyama have been family-owned for over 200 years, passing down traditional cultivation and processing techniques from father to son.

From Garden to Cup

Each tea is personally selected by our team as we travel throughout Shizuoka Prefecture. We hand-pick the finest leaves, process them using traditional methods, and package them fresh to bring you the authentic taste of Shizuoka. Our mission is to share the authentic taste and cultural heritage of Honyama and Shizuoka tea with tea lovers around the world.
